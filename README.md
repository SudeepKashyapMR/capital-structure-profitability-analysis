# capital-structure-profitability-analysis
Impact of Capital Structure on Profitability – Analysis of Indian Companies (2016–2025)
Project Overview

This project examines the relationship between capital structure and profitability among selected Indian companies across multiple sectors over a 10-year period (2016–2025).

Capital structure decisions—specifically the mix of debt and equity financing—play a critical role in determining a firm’s financial performance and risk profile. The objective of this project is to analyze how financial indicators such as Return on Equity (ROE), Sales, Equity, and Debt-to-Equity (D/E) ratio influence a company’s Net Profit Margin (NPM).

Using financial data from 20 major Indian companies, the study applies regression analysis and Power BI visualization to identify patterns and relationships between financing decisions and profitability.

This project is part of my MBA coursework in Finance and Analytics at PES University.

Research Objective

The main objectives of this study are:

• To examine the impact of capital structure on profitability of Indian companies.
• To analyze the relationship between Debt-to-Equity ratio and Net Profit Margin (NPM).
• To evaluate how firm-specific financial indicators such as ROE, Sales, and Equity influence profitability.
• To provide insights into optimal financing strategies for companies operating in different industries.

Research Hypothesis
Null Hypothesis (H₀)

Debt-to-Equity ratio has no significant impact on Net Profit Margin (NPM) of Indian companies.

Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

Debt-to-Equity ratio has a significant impact on Net Profit Margin (NPM) of Indian companies.

Methodology

To evaluate the impact of capital structure on profitability, multiple regression analysis was used.

Dependent Variable

Net Profit Margin (NPM)

Independent Variables

• Return on Equity (ROE)
• Sales
• Equity
• Debt-to-Equity Ratio (D/E)

Regression Model

NPM = β₀ + β₁(ROE) + β₂(Sales) + β₃(Equity) + β₄(D/E) + ε

Regression analysis was selected because it helps quantify the strength and direction of relationships between financial variables.

Key Statistical Results

• Multiple R = 0.66
Indicates a moderately strong relationship between predicted and actual profitability values.

• R² = 0.43
Approximately 43% of the variation in Net Profit Margin is explained by the independent variables.

• Adjusted R² = 0.42
Confirms that the model provides a reliable explanation without unnecessary predictors.

• Standard Error = 6.06
Shows a reasonable model fit for financial research involving multiple firms and industries.

Key Findings

The analysis reveals several important insights:

1. ROE strongly influences profitability

Companies generating higher returns for shareholders tend to maintain stronger profit margins.

2. Equity has a positive impact on profitability

Firms with stronger equity bases are more financially stable and perform better.

3. Sales do not always guarantee profitability

Higher revenues do not necessarily translate into higher margins due to operational costs and inefficiencies.

4. Debt can improve profitability up to an optimal level

Moderate use of debt can enhance profitability through leverage benefits, but excessive debt increases financial risk.

Business Implications
For Managers

Companies should maintain an optimal balance between debt and equity to maximize profitability while minimizing financial risk.

For Investors

Investors should evaluate capital structure indicators such as ROE and D/E ratio when selecting stable companies.

For Policymakers

Encouraging responsible corporate financing practices can support long-term financial stability in the corporate sector.
